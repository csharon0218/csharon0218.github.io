Conference Poster KSAS 2025 Fall Conference UAV Vision System

A Webots-based HILS Testbed for Low-cost UAV Vision Systems

저가형 UAV 비전 시스템을 위한 Webots 기반 HILS 테스트베드

Hoseon Gi, Daewon Gang, Jawoon Cho, Junsue Song, Minseo Choi, Wai Nwe Tun, Gilho Lee, and Inmo Jang

KSAS 2025 Fall Conference, Korea Society for Aeronautical and Space Sciences, 2025.

Summary

This work presents a Webots-based Hardware-in-the-Loop Simulation testbed for validating a low-cost UAV vision system under realistic hardware constraints. The system estimates the absolute coordinates of ground objects using a monocular camera, GPS information, YOLOv8n-based object detection, and geometric localization.

The HILS environment integrates a Webots simulator, a vision algorithm module, and MCU-based embedded boards to evaluate the real-time performance and accuracy of the proposed lightweight vision pipeline.

Keywords

HILS Monocular Vision Geometric Localization Embedded Systems UAV Webots

Key Contributions

  • Designed a lightweight object coordinate estimation pipeline for low-cost UAVs using monocular vision and GPS information.
  • Built a Webots-based HILS testbed to validate UAV vision algorithms under hardware-aware simulation conditions.
  • Evaluated the proposed pipeline on embedded computing boards, including Jetson Orin Nano, Raspberry Pi 5, and Coral Dev Board.
  • Demonstrated the practical feasibility of lightweight vision algorithms for real-time target localization and UAV vision system validation.

Research Relevance

This project strengthened my interest in connecting perception, simulation, and real-world robotic systems. It also provided a foundation for my research interests in embodied AI, multimodal perception, and physical AI systems.
